Our Team

About Our Team

Our team is multi-generational multi-talented and interfaith. Most of us are affiliated with the Egyptian Moral Rearmament Association (EMRA) which has been established and registered in the Egyptian Ministry of Social Welfare since March 1988, registration number 3563, and is a member of Initiatives of change international association (IofC) / MRA) registered in the UN since 2002.

EMRA aims to inspire positive change, from the personal to the global through trust building, compassion and caring. EMRA encourages research in related fields as well as translation of MRA/IofC works from English to Arabic and vice versa, as well as creative writing

EMRA encourages training and self-development in related fields in Egypt and internationally in collaboration with IofC international.

EMRA collaborates with national civil society associations that share its values and aspirations e.g. the Egyptian Council For Foreign Affairs (ECFA) , the Egyptian Association of University Women(EAUW) which is a member of the International Federation of University Women "IFUW" (GWI) ,the Council for Arab West Understanding (CAWU), Partners for Development and Al-Tawba Zakkat (Charity) and Community Development Committee registered in Nasser’s Social Bank Registration Number :2886 . It also collaborates with the Ministry of Egyptian Foreign Affairs and the Ministry of Youth as well as the Ministry of Culture.

EMRA is a member of the MRA/IofC Arab Coordination Group (ARCG) .EMRA is also looking forward to connect more closely with the IofC African Coordination Group (AFCG). EMRA has close ties with Initiatives of Change (IofC) international association registered in the United Nations.

EMRA employs multi-track diplomacy in the spirit of an alliance of human conscience promoting peace based on justice. This is achieved through exchange visits and participation in regional and international conferences, dialogues, forums, retreats and consultations.

EMRA cares for art as “A Tool for Change and Instrument of Peace”. Thus, encouraging hope revival and ethical artistic presentations and screening and discussion of inspiring movies and documentaries especially those produced by MRA/IofC “For The Love of Tomorrow Films” such as “The Imam and the Pastor” and “Beyond Forgiving”.

Synergy implies that the behavior of the whole is more than the sum of the parts.

Dr. Aida Abdel-Rahman

Dr. Aida Abdel-Rahman is an Egyptian scholar and educationist living in Los Angeles, California. She has a BA in French and Education from Ein Shams university, an MA in linguistics from La Sorbonne/ Paris, a PhD in education from Rutgers/Kensington, USA and a Diploma in written translation from Cairo university and Instant translation Al Alson. She taught French, Arabic and translation at high schools and university levels in many places including Princeton and Oran University. She has been evolved in curriculum development in several countries. She has a long and profound experience with interfaith dialogue as a means of community building

She summarizes her experience and convictions in the following words.

1- Building an Interfaith community opens us to a new understanding of the Divine. We learn through each other’s stories.

2- We also gain a better understanding of our own faith because we must now share and explain it to others. Interfaith should, in no way, threaten our faith but rather strengthen it.

3- The effort here is not to convert, but to gain a better understanding of other faiths.

4- Interfaith work helps us do amazing work as we join forces, which makes a larger impact in the communities we are serving and helps us bring about changes in transformative ways. It also strengthens our healing network in the face of difficulties and adversities

5- We find out that we are all common partners and advocates for social justice because we share the same goals and values thus we can stand up in partnership and solidarity against various phobias.

6- In standing together, it is important that we respect our differences, embrace our commonalities and break through polarization. We must lovingly lay out issues we disagree upon, and through the grace of God, come to a common solution in an effort to promote peace and justice.

Mrs. Ekbal Al Asyouti

Ekbal was born and raised in Egypt. Her Giurgius (George) Beshara Al Asyouti was a generous land lord who built a mosque for the peasants in his farm although he was a Christian and mounted framed verses from the holy Quran in his office where he met the peasants to discuss their affairs and concerns . When he passed away, her eldest brother Sammy expanded the mosque . it was originally name “Giurgius” mosque and then renamed when her brother expanded it it was named “Al-Salaam” mosque, meaning “peace” Sammy also built grave yards for the peasants who were expanding in number. Ekbal studied at the English Mission’s college which she considered the best school in the whole wide world.

After 1956 the tripartite aggression (Britain France and Isrel) on Egypt ithe school was renamed “ Al Salaam “ College Her B.A. was in English literature with very high grades and honours from Cairo university which made her eligible to join the AUC where she continued her post graduate studies. She joined Middle East News Agency (MENA) where she excelled in interviewing and meeting heads of states, prime ministers and dignitaries from all over the world including Nehro, Sukarno of Indonesia, Tito of Yugoslavia and and Nasser of Egypt ,leaders of the nonaligned movements as well as Indira Ghandi. Pierre Trudeau of Canada. She travelled to Canada where she joined the Egyptian embassy in Ottawa where she became the first woman press councilor in the contemnor history of Egypt. After her return to Egypt she rejoined MENA until she became 80 years old. She joined EMRA in 2001 when she traveled to Malta to participate in the Mediterranean Dialogue and 2002, 2004 to Caux Switzerland to participate in the human security Forum and accompany the Egyptian youth delegation and cast of the multimedia Egypt new hope. The trials of the Palestinian and Sudanese is very close to her heart and is always ready to support them. Her ardent prayers are for peace in our world.
